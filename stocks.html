What this actually says (read before picking)

  • "Don't get poorer" is easily cleared. Every broad index returned +7% to +12%/yr after inflation over 10 years — $10k → ~$40k (S&P 500) vs the ~$13k inflation line.
  • The S&P 500 beat the "diversified" mixes (15.4% vs ~10%) — but that's a US-led decade, not a law, and it fell harder (−34% vs −23%). More diversification = smoother, lower return.
  • Bonds lost to inflation (BND −1.3% real). "Safe" didn't preserve purchasing power this decade.
  • Individual stocks = uncompensated risk. The market doesn't pay you extra to hold one owner's hidden story — you could diversify it away for free. So don't carry it.
  • Temper expectations: this was an above-average bull (~15%/yr vs ~7% real long-run), measured near highs. Plan for ~7%/yr real and a ~35% drawdown sometime — the price of admission.
  • The move: one broad fund (VT, or VTI+VXUS), maybe a slice of bonds, auto-invest monthly, hold for years. No picking.
